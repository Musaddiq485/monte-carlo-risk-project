    "# Monte Carlo Simulation for Portfolio Market Risk Estimation\n",
    "\n",
    "## Overview\n",
    "\n",
    "This project applies Monte Carlo simulation to estimate portfolio market risk.  \n",
    "It extends a previous historical risk analysis by simulating thousands of possible future return scenarios.\n",
    "\n",
    "The goal is to evaluate potential losses and compute key risk measures such as Value-at-Risk (VaR) and Expected Shortfall (ES).\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "## Motivation\n",
    "\n",
    "Traditional risk models rely on historical data, but financial markets are uncertain.  \n",
    "Monte Carlo simulation allows us to model future scenarios and better understand potential extreme losses.\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "## Methodology\n",
    "\n",
    "### 1. Data Preparation\n",
    "- Historical price data\n",
    "- Log return computation\n",
    "- Equal-weight portfolio construction\n",
    "\n",
    "### 2. Parameter Estimation\n",
    "- Mean (μ) and volatility (σ) estimated from historical returns\n",
    "- Assumption: returns follow a normal distribution\n",
    "\n",
    "### 3. Monte Carlo Simulation\n",
    "- 10,000 simulated return scenarios\n",
    "- Generation of future portfolio returns using random sampling\n",
    "\n",
    "### 4. Risk Measures\n",
    "- Value-at-Risk (VaR 95% and 99%)\n",
    "- Expected Shortfall (ES)\n",
    "\n",
    "### 5. Visualization\n",
    "- Histogram of simulated loss distribution\n",
    "- VaR thresholds highlighted\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "## Key Results\n",
    "\n",
    "- Monte Carlo simulation provides a probabilistic view of future losses  \n",
    "- VaR estimates loss thresholds under extreme scenarios  \n",
    "- Expected Shortfall captures tail risk beyond VaR  \n",
    "- Results depend strongly on model assumptions  \n",
    "\n",
